Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to help you prepare for your visit.

For most patients, we recommend a routine check-up and cleaning every six months. This allows us to catch potential issues early and keep your teeth and gums in optimal health. Depending on your specific oral health needs, we may recommend a different schedule.

The American Academy of Pediatric Dentistry recommends a child's first visit occur by their first birthday. This early visit is a great way to establish a positive relationship with the dentist and ensure your child's dental development is on track.

If you experience a dental emergency such as a broken tooth, severe pain, or trauma, please contact our office as soon as possible. We prioritize emergency cases and will provide guidance on what to do and schedule a prompt appointment to address the issue.

Yes, we are always happy to welcome new individuals and families to our practice. You can schedule your first appointment by using the contact form on our website or by calling our office during business hours.

Your first visit will include a comprehensive examination of your teeth, gums, and mouth, as well as any necessary X-rays. We will discuss your dental history, listen to any concerns you may have, and work with you to create a personalized treatment plan. Most importantly, we'll take the time to get to know you.
